FAQ

REGISTRATION

Q. How much is admission?
Admission is free!

Q. How can I register?
All registration will be done on-site. There will be a registration desk outside of the Price Center West Ballroom for guests to check-in.

Q. I'm not a student at UCSD. Can I still attend?
Of course! We welcome students, alumni, and everyone else to our convention.

POLICIES

Q. Can I take pictures of other attendees and/or exhibits?
Photos and videos will be allowed throughout the event. However, we urge you to request permission before you take photos of any cosplayers, panelists, and Artist Alley participants.

Q. Are there any cosplay restrictions?
All cosplays must adhere to the following rules:
-- Cosplays must be appropriate and respectful.
-- Cosplays should not expose the body in such a way to be considered indecent exposure under California and San Diego ordinances.
-- Costumes that are realistic depictions of American Law Enforcement, American Military, or other emergency response personnel's uniforms are NOT permitted.

Q. What are the weapons policies?
All weapons must adhere to the following policies:
-- Weapons considered illegal on UCSD grounds, San Diego, California, and the United States are NOT allowed.
-- Real weapons, projectile weapons (guns, bows, etc), baseball bats, weapons with live steel, and any other weapons that may pose a danger to attendees are NOT allowed.
-- Prop weapons MUST be peace bonded by registration staff at the registration table. Staff will only peace bond weapons that relate to and enhance the cosplay that it's for.
-- Prop weapons must be inoperable.
-- All attendees with peace bonded weapons must adhere to strict behavioral standards. Weapons should not be brandished in any threatening manner or used in any way that could pose a threat to others. Weapons must also remain with the attendee who originally had it peace bonded.

GENERAL

Q. Can I re-enter the convention after I've left?
Yes, attendees may come and go as they please.

Q. I need to find a staff member during the convention. Who can I talk to?
All Cal Animage Beta officers/staff members will be wearing some sort of identifying symbol (TBD). There will also be a help desk near the entrance for any questions.

MISC

Q. What is Cal Animage Beta?
Cal Animage Beta is UCSD's one-and-only anime club. In addition to AnimeFest, we have weekly GBMs (held Monday evenings) where we watch anime. We also host various other quarterly and yearly events such as karaoke, Tea Party (where we marathon an entire anime series), and Beach Day.

Panels

Here is the full panel listing for AnimeFest 2016. Time, location, and description of each panel are included.


Friendship no Jutsu: An Examination of the "Shonen" Genre through Naruto - Brandon Nguyen

12 p.m. - Marshall College Room

You've probably seen or read a lot of "shonen" series, such as that one about everyone's favorite ninja Naruto. Ever noticed how they all seem to share some of the same themes? Friendship and rivalry, family and teamwork, or everyone having unique powers. If you are interested in taking a look at shonen series with a more discerning eye, come to my discussion of the genre through the lens of one of the "Big Three" shonen series in recent memory: Naruto. Also come if you have no idea what shonen is, we'll go over that too.

Jungle Emperor vs. the Lion King: Theories and Techniques of Animation - Professor Daisuke Miyao

1 p.m. - Marshall College Room

UCSD professor Daisuke Miyao presents a panel comparatively discussing Tezuka Osamu's Jungle Emperor and Disney's The Lion King in terms of their styles. The issues to be examined include 2-D vs. 3-D, limited animation vs. full animation, and Japan vs. Hollywood.

Intro to Lolita Fashion - Shannon Subers

3 p.m. - Marshall College Room

Have you heard of lolita fashion but don't know much about it? Would you like to learn more about this cute style? Are you already a lolita looking to find some more frilly friends? Join us for a brief overview of what lolita is all about, a live demo on how to build an outfit, resources on where to purchase clothes, and the Ask-A-Lolita Q&A! Previous exposure to Japanese street fashion not required-- newbies welcome!

Figure Collecting 101 - Jesus Guerra Fematt Jr.

4 p.m. - Marshall College Room

Figure collecting 101 goes over the basics of getting into figure collecting. I'll be discussing the terminology/classifications within figure collecting community, a rundown of the various figure companies from a collector's perspective, and where to procure officially licensed figures. Its educational and light, you'll get to see all sorts of upcoming figures, how to watch out for bootlegs, and see how the figure collecting community has fun, Q&A time will be towards the end for those who want to delve into the specifics.

Intro to Fighting Games - Alfonso Acosta and Jesus Guerra Fematt Jr.

12:30 p.m. - Revelle College Room

We'll be going over the fundamentals of fighting games, a lesson which will include teaching a basic understanding of neutral game, frames, command inputs and more. The idea of the panel is to craft the foundation for your trip to becoming a fighting game contender, finding the perfect main character, your ideal play style, learning the concepts and applying them to your arsenal. Outside of informing you on how to improve your fighting game skills, we'll also be pointing you in the way of cool resources within the community. We'll also have a Q&A section along with a live demonstration of the concepts shown within the panel.

UC Teitokus - Patrick Casis

1:30 p.m. - Revelle College Room

Kantai Collection or Kancolle for short is a Japanese free-to-play web browser game developed by Kadokawa Games. Set in a world where humanity has lost control of the oceans to the "deep sea fleet," the only hope to counter this threat are the Kanmusu, a group of girls who possess the spirit of Japanese warships. Come and learn how join the world of Kantai Collection/Kancolle and assemble your fleet and defeat the "deep see fleet" terrorizing the world.

Anime and You: Having Fun with Tropes - Jericho Cabanayan

2:30 p.m. - Revelle College Room

Storytelling has been around for ages, bringing entertainment for people generation after generation. Various mediums have been used, but anime is one of the most prevalent modes of storytelling today. So this time around, I will be discussing popular anime cliches and character personalities. We will look at examples such as "accidental perversion" and "love bubbles." We will learn about tsunderes, yanderes, and the legendary thugdere. This will be an exciting workshop, complete with an opportunity to write our own stories with the tropes we learned from the panel.
